# Resources
This is a short summary of useful resources for this project.

## Quantum Monte Carlo
[Quantum Monte Carlo Simulation of Solids, 
W. M. C. Foulkes, L. Mitas, R. J. Needs, and G. Rajagopal
Rev. Mod. Phys. 73, 33 – Published 5 January 2001](https://journals.aps.org/rmp/abstract/10.1103/RevModPhys.73.33) is
a good first introduction to monte carlo simulations for quantum systems.  This is a good place to start before
introducing the nuclear problem.

## Nuclear QMC
The following are useful introductions:
* [Quantum Monte Carlo methods for nuclear physics, J. Carlson et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/1412.3081)
* [Atomic nuclei from quantum Monte Carlo calculations with chiral EFT interactions, S. Gandolfi et al.](
https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.01374
) 

## EFT
* [Two- and three-nucleon contact interactions and ground-state energies of light- andmedium-mass nuclei](
https://arxiv.org/pdf/2102.02327.pdf) EFT up to N3LO.

## Nuclear ML
* [Variational Monte Carlo calculations of A≤4 nuclei with an artificial neural-network correlator ansatz](
https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.14282
)
* [Nuclei with up to A=6 nucleons with artificial neural network wave functions](
https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.06836
)
